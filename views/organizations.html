Harvard Square Homeless Shelter

The Street Team is a fully incorporated program of the Harvard Square Homeless Shelter, consisting of 21 trained volunteers, that runs from November 1st to April 15th. Each night, a group of 3 volunteers arrives at the Shelter around 7:45 to prepare sandwiches and pack up other food before going out into Harvard Square to provide individuals with basic amenities such as food, blankets, and socks; distribute information about available resources (i.e. healthcare, job searches, food during the day, etc.); refer individuals to local shelters; and build relationships with people sleeping outside. The commitment for Street Team consists of initial training, participating in one weekly night shift from 7:45 PM to 10:00 PM, and coming to an hour-long meeting twice a month. Street Team shifts are a low time commitment, but they have high rewards â€” shifts can be incredible and sometimes draining despite their shortness. Generally drawing on student volunteers with no prior experience with the homeless, Street Team looks only for enthusiasm, compassion, and a willingness to brave the cold.

Animal Rescue League of Boston

Boston Lobby Volunteer: This volunteer position works with our adoption center in the lobby area of our Boston headquarters. We are in need of help greeting clients, directing clients to the appropriate location, answering incoming phone calls and other light administrative duties. This volunteer would also be responsible for light housekeeping duties to help keep the lobby area presentable. This position is available on nights and weekends.

Inspire Future Engineers at the Junior Solar Sprint

Join us as middle school students from across Boston compete in the annual Junior Solar Sprint! Over 50 student teams have been designing and building model solar-powered cars this spring, led by STEM professionals who volunteer their time as teachers. Students will compete to find the best-designed and fastest car. WE NEED YOU to help students see the value and potential of their efforts by volunteering at the Junior Solar Sprint. This is a great opportunity to help young people connect their effort and creativity with a sense of opportunity in the future!
